# Budget Planner — Quick Start Guide

Welcome. This is a phone-first budgeting app that runs right in your browser and
keeps your money data in **your own Google Sheet** (or fully offline on your
device). No accounts to create, no monthly fee, nothing to install.

This guide is short on purpose. You can start using the app in about two minutes.

---

## 1. Open it and look around

The app opens straight to your **Dashboard**. On first visit you're in **Demo
mode** — the app is filled with realistic sample data so you can tap around and
see how everything looks before entering anything of your own.

- On a phone: the row of icons along the bottom moves you between sections.
- On a computer: a sidebar on the left does the same.
- See the little **compass** at the top of any screen? Tap it for a 20-second
  guided tour of just that screen. You can replay it any time.

When you're ready to use your own numbers, open **Settings** and tap
**"Use my own data."** Demo data is never saved and never mixed with yours.

---

## 2. Set up your budget (2 minutes)

The fastest way in is the **Quick Setup** wizard — it appears automatically the
first time you open **Budget** with your own data (or tap **Quick setup** on the
empty Budget screen). Four short steps:

1. **Currency** — pick your symbol.
2. **Income** — add each paycheck.
3. **Bills** — tick the ones you have (rent, phone, internet…) and adjust amounts.
4. **Starting balance** — what's in your main account right now.

That's it — you'll land on a working budget with "left to spend" already
calculated. Everything after this is optional and adds more detail.

---

## 3. The main screens

You don't need all of these. Start with **Budget** and add the rest as you go.

| Screen | What it's for |
| --- | --- |
| **Dashboard** | Your key numbers at a glance: left to spend, bills due, saved, owed. |
| **Budget** | This period's plan vs. what actually happened. Add income, bills, expenses. |
| **Transactions** | A dated log of every dollar in and out. |
| **Recurring** | Set a bill or paycheck once; it repeats on schedule automatically. |
| **Calendar** | Every payment on a month view, with each day's total. |
| **Savings** | Sinking funds — save toward specific goals with progress rings. |
| **Debt Payoff** | Snowball or avalanche plan, with your debt-free date. |
| **Net Worth** | Everything you own minus everything you owe, over time. |
| **50/30/20** | Are your Needs / Wants / Savings in balance? |
| **Distribution** | For couples/families: who earns and who spends. |
| **Annual** | A rolling 12-month view of the whole year. |

Using fewer of these? **Settings → Customize sections** hides the ones you don't
want, so your menu stays tidy. Nothing is deleted.

---

## 4. Back up to your own Google Sheet (recommended)

By default your data lives only in this browser. Connecting a Google Sheet gives
you a real backup that's **yours**, and lets you open the same budget on your
phone and computer.

1. Open **Settings → Google Sheets → Connect Google**.
2. Choose your Google account and allow access.

The app creates one spreadsheet in your Google Drive and keeps it in sync
automatically after that. It only ever touches the one file it created — it can't
see the rest of your Drive.

**On a second device:** connect the same Google account, or paste your sheet's
link into **Settings → Link an existing sheet**, and your budget appears.

---

## 5. Import from your bank (optional)

Already have transactions in your bank's export? Don't retype them.

1. **Transactions → Import from bank CSV.**
2. Paste the CSV text, or choose the downloaded `.csv` file.
3. The app auto-detects the date, amount, and description columns — adjust if
   needed.
4. Review the list: likely duplicates are flagged and unticked for you. Untick
   anything else you don't want, then **Import**.

It all happens on your device — nothing is uploaded anywhere.

---

## 6. Sharing with a partner or family

**Settings → Household** lets you add the people who share the budget. Their names
then appear as one-tap choices whenever you tag who earned or spent something, and
they power the **Distribution** screen so you can see each person's share.

*"Family Shared"* is always available for shared expenses.

---

## Handy to know

- **It works offline.** Everything keeps working with no internet; it syncs when
  you're back online (if you connected a sheet).
- **Update the app:** it refreshes itself, but you can force it from
  **Settings → Check for updates**.
- **Charts and rings** update the instant you log something — no refresh needed.
- **Gentle by design:** overdue bills are worded kindly, and there are no
  notification firehoses.

That's everything you need to start. Explore at your own pace — the compass on
each screen is always there if you want a refresher.
